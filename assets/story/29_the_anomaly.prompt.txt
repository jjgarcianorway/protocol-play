Deep space. The ark ship Aurora seen from very far away — a tiny point of warm light, fragile, alone in the immense darkness. The ship is small enough to feel vulnerable but bright enough to feel alive. Around it, ahead of it, space itself seems to pulse — subtle concentric waves emanating from a point in the distance, like ripples spreading outward in still water, but made of bent starlight. The waves are not threatening. They feel organic, almost like breathing — a slow, vast inhale and exhale in the fabric of space. Stars behind the waves are gently distorted, stretched and compressed in soft patterns. The distortion has a faint luminescence, the barest hint of blue-teal light at the edges of each wave. The Aurora is approaching this phenomenon, drifting toward the edge of the first ripple. The composition emphasizes scale: the ship is tiny, the waves are enormous, and whatever is generating them is beyond the frame. Style: low-poly 3D, soft cinematic lighting, matching protocol: play aesthetic. Color palette: deep space blacks and navy blues, the Aurora as a warm amber point, the waves in subtle teals and pale blues, distant stars as cool white pinpoints. Stylized, evocative — NOT photorealistic. Should make the player FEEL, not just see. Mood: cosmic wonder, the edge of understanding — not fear but awe, the feeling of standing at the threshold of something that rewrites what you thought was possible.
