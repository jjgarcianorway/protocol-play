Deep space, infinite black dotted with distant stars. In the centre of the frame, a barely visible distortion in the starfield — like heat haze rising from asphalt, but in the void. Stars behind the distortion are slightly bent, shifted, as if space itself is breathing. The distortion has no defined edge, no colour, just a wrongness in the geometry of the universe. In the far corner, the faint silhouette of the Aurora's hull, sensors pointing toward the anomaly. Style: photorealistic astrophotography meets cosmic horror. Colors: the black of deep space, pinpoint white stars, the distortion is visible only by what it bends. Mood: unease, wonder, the vertigo of encountering something that shouldn't exist.
