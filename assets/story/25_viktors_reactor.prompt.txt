A man alone in a reactor control room in the dead of night. Multiple screens surround him, casting his face in pale blue-white light — each screen showing reactor schematics, power curves, containment diagrams. A digital clock on the wall reads 4:17 AM. Viktor sits hunched forward, one hand on a keyboard, the other pressed against his temple. His face is tired, shadows under his eyes, but his gaze is sharp, still working, still solving. He hasn't gone home in days. Through the reinforced window behind him, the faint pulsing glow of a fusion reactor core — warm amber light, almost organic, like a heartbeat made of energy. The control room is cluttered: coffee cups, printouts, a photo pinned to a monitor edge (too small to see clearly). The contrast between the cold blue screens and the warm reactor glow behind him tells the whole story. Style: low-poly 3D, soft cinematic lighting, matching protocol: play aesthetic. Color palette: cold blues and teals from the screens, warm amber from the reactor glow, dark shadows. Stylized, evocative — NOT photorealistic. Should make the player FEEL, not just see. Mood: guilt transmuted into purpose — a haunted man who cannot undo the past but will not stop trying to build the future.
